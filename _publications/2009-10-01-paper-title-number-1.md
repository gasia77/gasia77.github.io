excerpt: 'In cooperative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL), agents attempt to complete the same task, such as defeating enemies and scoring goals. Due to the trial-and-error mechanism, MARL algorithms usually require a large amount of interactions with the environment to achieve convergence. Although a heuristic-guided framework has been proposed for acceleration, challenges persist in acquiring suitable prior knowledge or offline data, impeding efficient policy learning. To address this problem, we propose Heuristic Memory Utilization (HMU) and its adaptive variant for MARL, a novel heuristic algorithm. HMU incorporates a feature extractor encoder to generate latent representations with self-predictive characteristics, so as to identify states with analogous properties in low-dimensional space more effectively. Agents will receive heuristic information of similar states based on the resemblance between latent representations to accelerate learning and avoid falling in local optima. The proposed methods are evaluated on SMAC II and Google Research Football, and experimental results indicate they outperform the state-of-the-art methods.'
